**SSIP isn't a scheme you join — it's the umbrella that makes the others interchangeable.** SSIP (Safety Schemes in Procurement) keeps the core health & safety assessment consistent across more than 50 UK member schemes, so a single approved certificate is accepted everywhere. Understand SSIP and you stop paying for duplicate assessments.

**TL;DR:** SSIP is an umbrella body whose 50+ member schemes (CHAS, SafeContractor, SMAS, Constructionline and others) all assess against the same core health & safety criteria. Deem to Satisfy mutual recognition means one approved certificate satisfies SSIP requirements across all members — so you only pay for the cheapest scheme that fits your size. SSIP covers health & safety only: it does not include financial vetting, the Common Assessment Standard, or client-specific PQQs. Hold the cheapest SSIP-approved scheme and you're done for the health & safety gate; pay more only if you need the broader layers.

## What SSIP actually is

SSIP is a forum/umbrella body. Its members — [CHAS, SafeContractor and Constructionline](/compare/chas-vs-safecontractor-vs-constructionline), SMAS, and around 50 others — all assess contractors against the same **SSIP Core Criteria**, which align with HSE guidance. The scope is **health & safety only**.

That shared standard is what enables mutual recognition: because every member assesses against the same criteria, your certificate from one can be accepted by another.

## Deem to Satisfy: one assessment, recognised across members

**Deem to Satisfy (DtS)** is the agreement that lets a valid SSIP certificate from one member be accepted by other members **without re-assessment**. SSIP reports that over £10 million was saved in 2023 alone through reduced duplicate assessments.

We cover exactly how to use it — and its limits — in [SSIP mutual recognition explained](/ssip/ssip-mutual-recognition-explained). If you've seen the name **Acclaim** change, that scheme is now Once For All Health & Safety — see [Acclaim is now Once For All Health & Safety](/ssip/acclaim-once-for-all-ssip).
